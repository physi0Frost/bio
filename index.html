Clinical Precision, Revolutionary Purpose

M d. Rifatur Rahman is a Clinical Physiotherapist operating at the intersection of evidence-based practice and mission-driven reform. His professional life is defined by two distinct, yet united, goals: delivering high-level clinical care and actively dismantling the barriers that limit access to quality physiotherapy education.

As a Clinical Physiotherapist in the Musculoskeletal Unit at the Centre for the Rehabilitation of the Paralysed (CRP), Rifatur manages complex orthopedic, spine, and musculoskeletal caseloads. His practice is built on a foundation of advanced postgraduate specialization. He is highly trained in Mechanical Diagnosis and Therapy (MDT - McKenzie Method), managing complex MSK pain, and Dry Needling. This specialized expertise allows him to apply precise differential diagnosis and advanced manual therapy, with further clinical experience extending into specialized burn rehabilitation units.

"I believe motion is hope. I believe healing is a form of rebellion."

However, clinical practice is only half of the equation. Rifatur is driven by a restless academic curiosity, viewing research not as a separate pursuit but as a necessary component of excellence. His belief that physiotherapy in Bangladesh must evolve through data and population-specific insight is reflected in his Master’s thesis on "Effectiveness of Reciprocal Inhibition Techniques for Individuals with Adhesive Capsulitis of Shoulder."

This drive extends beyond the clinic. Rifatur is the founder of Motion Mechanics Physiotherapy and Rehabilitation Hub, a platform born from a new generation's demand for fairness, transparency, and purpose. Motion Mechanics is his answer to a system he seeks to change; it's an engine built to "restore knowledge culture, break gatekeeping, and bring global-standard physiotherapy education within reach of every student in Bangladesh."

Ultimately, Rifatur's career is a unified narrative. Anchored by his role as a father to three children, he mentors junior physiotherapists, contributes to research, and builds a digital community—all driven by a single philosophy. He is working to move Bangladesh’s physiotherapy landscape closer to the future it deserves.
